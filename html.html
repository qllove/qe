John Peter Hussman （born 15 October 1962） 1.American philanthropist, economist, and hedge fund manager. 2.Holds a PhD in economics from Stanford University, as well as a master's degree in education and social policy and a bachelor's degree in economics from Northwestern University. 3.In the mid-1980s, Hussman worked as an options mathematician for Peters & Company at the Chicago Board of Trade, and in 1988 founded his investment company, now known as Hussman Strategic Advisors. 4.From 1992 to 1998, he was Professor of Economics and International Finance at the University of Michigan. 5.Successfully anticipated the collapse of dot-com bubble, as well as the 2008–09 global financial crisis. 6.In 1998, Hussman left academic economics to increase his focus on philanthropic efforts, using his ongoing investing work to fund charitable projects in autism, global health, education, displaced populations, homelessness, and research focused on the genetics and biology of complex conditions such as autism and multiple sclerosis. 7.Hussman was chosen as the 2013 Philanthropist of the Year by the AFP.
